# Sales Model A — Package + Commercial License

## What's for sale

The Flutter package in `/package` is **MIT-licensed and free to use**, including in commercial applications, with no attribution requirement.

The "Commercial License" is a separate legal document for organizations that need:

- Formal vendor terms (legal team-friendly).
- Indemnification clauses.
- A named support contact and SLA.
- Priority feature requests (e.g. custom gateway adapter).

The MIT package gives a startup or solo dev everything they need. The commercial license is bought by larger orgs whose procurement teams require it.

## Distribution channels

- **pub.dev**: free MIT-licensed package, primary discovery surface.
- **GitHub Sponsors**: passive donation funnel from happy users.
- **Direct landing**: `marketing-site/` Stripe-checkout form for commercial tiers.
- **Cold outreach**: LinkedIn → MENA SaaS founders (~20 leads/week sustainable).

## Why anyone would buy when MIT is free

1. **Procurement**: Large companies (esp. financial institutions, hospitals, government) cannot consume MIT packages without a signed commercial agreement.
2. **Priority issues**: bug reports on the commercial tier jump the queue.
3. **Custom gateway**: enterprise tier includes 8 hours of custom adapter development per year.
4. **SLA**: response time guaranteed (4h indie, 1h studio, 30m enterprise).
5. **Indemnification**: legal coverage if the package causes an outage (capped at license fee × 10).

## Sales motion

- **Inbound**: support email → discovery call → license quote.

## Upgrade path

Customers buying Indie Commercial routinely upgrade to Studio after their next product launch. Studio customers upgrade to Enterprise after their second year.

Pricing model:

- Indie → Studio: pay difference ($300).
- Studio → Enterprise: pay difference ($500) + annual fee renews.
